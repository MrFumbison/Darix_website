TorIX Peers

There are more than 250 organizations with unique autonomous system numbers at the Toronto Internet Exchange.  While many are Internet & Network Service Providers, a wide diversity of other types of organizations is well represented here.

From the biggest content, hosting and cloud companies, to enterprises, critical DNS infrastructure providers, regional educational institutions or gaming companies, TorIX provides the network fabric which allow each to reach the resources they need quickly, securely and cost effectively.

At our scale, the administrative challenges of maintaining so many BGP peering sessions can seem daunting.  Over 80% of peers utilize our two BGP route-servers to easily exchange routing information with each other, and our route-servers have an extensive BGP community support to help organizations further control the distribution of their IP prefixes should they desire to.

Types of Organizations at TorIX

Peering Status Definitions

Accepting
Organization has an open peering policy and will likely peer with most other organizations at TorIX.
Conditional
Organization has selective peering policies which must be met before direct peering will be considered.
Connecting
Organization is in the process of connecting.
Not Accepting
Organization is not accepting peering requests at this time.
